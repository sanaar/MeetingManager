How is the Value Score calculated?
A transparent breakdown of what goes into your score

Your current score: 62 / 100 — Medium
The Value Score measures how much this meeting justifies the time it takes. It's made up of four equally weighted dimensions, each scored and combined into a single number.
The four dimensions
🎯
Focus
5/20
Measures how clear and specific your meeting goal is. Vague or broad topics score lower — a tightly scoped agenda with a single decision point scores highest.
Agenda clarity Single vs. multi-topic Defined outcome
🤝
Collaboration
10/16
Evaluates whether the right people are in the room. Too many attendees dilute focus; too few means key decisions can't be made. Scores highest when attendance is purposeful.
Attendee relevance Decision authority Group size
⏱
Time
15/20
Compares the meeting duration against the complexity of the goal. A 60-minute meeting for a simple status update scores low; a 30-minute focused decision session scores high.
Duration vs. goal complexity Calendar cost
⚖️
Balance
12/15
Considers whether this meeting could be replaced by a lighter-weight alternative — an email, async document, or quick Slack thread. Higher scores mean a meeting is genuinely the best format.
